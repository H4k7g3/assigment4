History of AC Milan

AC Milan was founded in 1899 by a British native, Alfred Edwards[1]. The club was named in honor of the city of the same name (in Italian, Milan is pronounced "Milano"). The team's first coach was Herbert Kilpin. It was he who came up with the shape of the club and its colors: black and red. The red color symbolized the devil, the patron of the team, and the black color symbolized the danger that threatened all his rivals, which is why the team began to be called the "Devils". The emblem of the club was a shield with the coat of arms of the city of Milan - a purple cross on a white field.
On March 11, 1900, Milan played their first unofficial match. The rivals were representatives of another Milan club - Mediolanum (where Herbert Kilpin had previously played). The squad for this match was as follows: Hood, Cagnani, Toretto, Fox, Kilpin, Valerio, Dubini, Davis, Neville, Allison, Formenti. Milan's confident 3-0 victory inspired the team, and they decided to join the Italian Football Federation, which gave them the right to play in the national championship.
